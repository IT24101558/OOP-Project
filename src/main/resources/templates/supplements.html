-------------------------------------- Foods --------------------------------------

Beef

Among the powerful package of 10 essential nutrients beef is known for, protein may be one of the most notable. In fact, one 3 oz cooked serving of beef provides 50% of your Daily Value (25 grams) of this important nutrient—making it an excellent source!.

  • Helps prevent anemia by boosting red blood cell production.

  • A great source of creatine and amino acids for bodybuilders.

  • Omega-3 fatty acids and iron support cognitive health.

  • Helps promote muscle growth and strength.

Eggs

As a general guideline, 2-3 whole eggs per day can provide 12-18 grams of high-quality protein, which can help meet the protein needs for many adults. However, the exact number will depend on the individual's specific protein requirements.


  • Contains all essential amino acids for muscle growth and repair.

  • Helps delay muscle fatigue, allowing for longer and stronger workouts.

  • Increases HDL (good cholesterol) for heart health.


Chicken

Chicken comes in a variety of cuts, providing between 24 and 32 grams of protein per 100 grams, depending on the cut. All chicken is high in protein, breasts providing the highest protein count. Chicken is one of the most commonly consumed meats worldwide.

  • Essential for muscle building, weight loss, and metabolism.

  • Helps maintain stable glucose levels when paired with healthy carbs.

  • Selenium and zinc help fight infections and improve immune response.

---------------------------------- Drinks ----------------------------------

Milk

Milk has about 8 grams of protein per cup. This is a good amount of protein for most people and can help to meet daily protein needs. Milk is also a source of other essential nutrients, such as calcium and vitamin D.

  • Contains potassium, which helps regulate blood pressure.

  • Provides carbohydrates, fats, and proteins for sustained energy levels.

  • Contains high-quality protein (casein & whey) essential for muscle repair.

  • Helps keep the body hydrated with its high water content.

Banana Juice

Bananas are a good source of nutrients, including some protein, banana juice, especially when made with added ingredients like milk or other fruits, can increase the protein content, offering a good source of protein and other nutrients

  • Contains essential minerals like magnesium and calcium, which support strong bones.

  • High in fiber and helps relieve constipation and promote gut health.

  • Tryptophan in bananas helps produce serotonin, improving mood and reducing stress.

  • Vitamin C and antioxidants improve immune system function.

Watermelon Juice

Watermelon juice contains a small amount of protein, with roughly 0.5 to 1 gram per cup, but watermelon seed kernels are significantly higher in protein.



  • High in fiber and water content, promoting a healthy digestive system.

  • Contains lycopene, an antioxidant that helps reduce the risk of heart disease.

  • Antioxidants like lycopene and cucurbitacin E have anti-inflammatory properties.

  • Boosts testosterone levels.
